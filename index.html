Chapter 01 — Foundation
Brand Foundation
品牌基礎
Owner: GPS Strategy & DX · Approver: GPS Director

Core Belief 核心信念

"When people feel genuinely safe — not just at work, but across their whole lives — they are free to focus, create, and contribute at their highest level."

This belief is GPS's commitment — the reason this strategy exists. Everything we communicate, every tool we propose, every change we introduce must trace back to it. It is not a slogan. It is the standard against which every decision is measured.

Narrative Architecture 敘事架構

To honour that commitment, all change streams — AI adoption, brand transformation, operational evolution — must be narrated through a single coherent arc, not run as separate campaigns. A unified narrative reduces cognitive load, builds cumulative meaning, and gives every initiative a clear place in a larger story.

We define two phases as our near-term direction. Beyond that, the strategy does not end — it iterates. Stakeholder feedback gathered at the close of each phase shapes the next: its priorities, its narrative emphasis, and its channel mix.

Brand Purpose 品牌目的

To transform physical security from a compliance function into a human-centered enabling force — making safety invisible, intuitive, and deeply personal across every site where our people work.

Brand Mission 品牌使命

Protect the world's most advanced semiconductor operations and the people within them by designing security systems that are precise, adaptive, and grounded in care.

Brand Vision 品牌願景

A world where security is not felt as restriction but experienced as freedom — where every person entering our sites carries the confidence that their safety has been designed in advance.

Five Brand Pillars 五大品牌支柱

Designed Safety
Security by design, not afterthought
Journey Protection
From home to site to home
Family Extension
Safety travels home with you
Living Culture
Safety as collective identity
Transparent Technology
Technology serving people visibly

Phased Narrative Arc 敘事弧線

Phase 1 · 2026

"We're changing how safety feels"

Challenge the assumption that security means restriction. Introduce new tools and ideas as expressions of care and support — for staff, for families, for the whole person. Establish a shared language across all sites.

Phase 2 · 2027

"Safety becomes who we are"

Transition from awareness to identity. GPS values are internalized by managers and reflected in day-to-day decisions — not only in formal communications. Cross-site communities of practice emerge.

Beyond Phase 2 — Continuous Iteration

Each phase closes with a structured review of stakeholder feedback — from security managers, site operations, partner functions, and contracted personnel. What we learn shapes the next phase: its priorities, its narrative emphasis, and its channel mix. The strategy improves because the people it serves inform it.

Philosophical Foundation 哲學基底

CPTED — Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design. GPS's approach is rooted in designing environments that inherently reduce risk rather than relying solely on enforcement. This philosophy informs our visual language: open sightlines, natural surveillance, and environments that feel welcoming because they are secure.

Chapter 02 — Foundation
Brand Behaviors
品牌行為準則
Owner: GPS Strategy & DX · Approver: GPS Director

Brand behaviors define how GPS people act — observable actions that any colleague or visitor should recognize within the first 10 minutes at a GPS-managed site.

Behavior 01
Explain Before Enforce
Every security procedure has a reason. We communicate the "why" before enforcing the "what."
Behavior 02
Greet First, Verify Second
Human acknowledgment precedes security protocol. A greeting before badge verification signals we protect people, not perimeters.
Behavior 03
Design for the Uncomfortable Moment
Security encounters are asymmetric. We proactively minimize discomfort, wait time, and ambiguity.
Behavior 04
Share What We Know
Safety knowledge is not proprietary. When GPS learns something that protects someone's family or commute — we share it.
Behavior 05 — Foundation
Treat Every Site as Someone's First Day
New hires, visitors, and transferred employees experience GPS before any other function. We are the first impression. Every interaction should meet the standard we would want for our own first day.

Measurement

Quarterly mystery visitor assessments, biannual safety perception survey (5 questions), and front-line supervisor peer reviews during shift handovers. Results feed into Brand Performance Metrics (Section 24).

Clearspace 安全空間

Always keep a minimum amount of clear space around the logo — free from text, graphics, and other elements. This minimum clearspace scales with the size of the logo mark and should never be reduced.

Scalability 尺寸規範

The logo was designed and tested to work at various sizes. Never use the logo smaller than the minimum sizes defined above. Below the standard minimum, an optical version of the logo is available with adjusted stroke weights and spacing to preserve legibility. Vertical lockups fall back to the horizontal optical logo at smaller scales.

Logo Misuse 標誌誤用

The appearance of the logo must remain consistent. It should not be reinterpreted, modified, or embellished. No attempt should be made to alter the logo in any way.

Misuse 01
Do not resize the mark
The mark and logotype must always scale together proportionally. Never enlarge or shrink the mark independently of the text.
Misuse 02
Do not rotate the logo
The logo must always appear in its standard upright orientation. No angular rotation is permitted.
Misuse 03
Do not change the color of the mark alone
The mark and text must share the same color treatment. Do not recolor the globe independently.
Misuse 04
Do not outline the logo
The logo uses solid fills. Do not convert to outline, add strokes, or apply border effects.
Misuse 05
Do not add gradients to the logotype
The gradient belongs to brand surfaces, not the logotype itself. Keep the text portion single-color.
Misuse 06
Do not stretch or manipulate
Never distort, warp, squeeze, or disproportionately scale the logo. Maintain exact aspect ratios at all times.

Logo Clarity 標誌清晰度

When creating a composition, it is important to have enough contrast between background and logo to maximize legibility.

DoUse the standard color logo on clean, light backgrounds. Use the white (reversed) logo on solid dark backgrounds. Ensure the background provides sufficient contrast — light solids, subtle gradients, or clear photographic areas all work.
Don'tDo not place the standard logo on same-tone colored backgrounds where contrast is insufficient. Do not place the reversed logo on busy, detailed, or low-contrast photographic backgrounds where the mark becomes illegible.

Type on Image 文字於影像上

Typography can be applied on top of imagery. In these instances, use black typography on lighter images, and white typography on darker images to ensure that there is enough contrast. Never use colored typography on imagery.

DoUse white text over subtle dark overlay to ensure readability. Use black text on clean, light backgrounds. Always meet WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body text).
Don'tAvoid placing light text on bright or detailed backgrounds. Do not use colored text over photos — it reduces legibility and looks unprofessional. Never sacrifice contrast for aesthetic preference.
Chapter 10 — Visual Identity
Graphic System & Iconography
圖形系統與圖標學
Owner: MarCom Team

GPS's graphic elements are derived from the logo mark's grid structure and curved form, echoing the brand's themes of protection, flow, and precision. These elements are designed to be used in conjunction with the brand logo, color, typography, and imagery. They can be applied in both sharp and blurred styles to introduce depth, motion, or emphasis, and are intended to complement layouts.

Graphic Elements — Core Visual

The primary graphic element is the checkerboard pattern derived from the logo mark, rendered on brand gradient backgrounds. These elements tie together the identity components — logo, typography, and imagery — into a cohesive visual system.

Three Element Styles 三種元素風格

Visual elements derived from the GPS logomark's grid structure and curved form can be applied in three distinct styles.

Style 01
Blurred Organic
Soft, atmospheric renderings of the checkerboard dots with gaussian blur and transparency. Used for background textures, hero sections, and brand-building materials where a subtle, modern feel is desired.
Style 02
Flowing Organic
Curved, dynamic arrangements of dots and shapes suggesting movement and protection. Used for event materials, campaign visuals, and content where energy and motion are desired.
Style 03
Sharp Geometric
Crisp, grid-aligned checkerboard patterns with clear edges. Used for data-driven content, technical documents, signage, and materials requiring precision and structure.

Graphic Application 圖形應用

DoUse graphic elements for general layout enhancement on brand gradient backgrounds. Use as appropriate repeated patterns for texture and depth. Use for highlight moments — accent corners, section dividers, and focal points alongside photography.
Don'tDo not overuse the graphics — excessive pattern coverage overwhelms content and reduces readability. Elements should complement, never compete with, the content they accompany. Maintain visual breathing room.

Icon System

All icons must be custom SVG — no icon libraries (FontAwesome, Material, etc.).

Imagery & Photography
影像與攝影
Owner: MarCom Team

Imagery Guide

Imagery should reflect GPS's core values of security, resilience, and human impact. Use authentic, professional photos that feature people in real environments — such as security checkpoints, or secure facilities — to convey trust and clarity. Supporting imagery may include architectural shots, surveillance technology, or abstract compositions that suggest protection and precision.

Lighting
Natural & Even
No dramatic shadows or excessive lens flare
Composition
Clear & Intentional
Strong focal point, clean backgrounds
People
Authentic
Real interactions, no staging or artificial feel
Color
Neutral
No oversaturation or high-contrast grading

All imagery is internal use only. Photos with identifiable individuals require signed release forms. The images are not authorized for external use.

Things to Avoid 應避免事項

Avoid Staging
Avoid staged or artificial feel. Prioritize genuine moments reflecting real interactions and scenarios. Do not include people in unnatural or staged poses.
Avoid Overproduction
Balance professionalism and visual appeal, avoiding excessive manipulation or editing. Do not use overly dynamic lighting and excessive lens flare. Do not use oversaturated or high-contrast color grading.
Avoid Disconnected Context
Clarify image context to match intended message and prevent confusion or misinterpretation. Avoid images with excessive post-production effects.
Avoid Poor Composition
Avoid clutter or chaos that diverts from intended focus. Ensure every photo has a clear subject and visual hierarchy.

Crisis Communication Protocol 危機溝通協議

When a security incident occurs at a GPS-managed site, brand voice must remain consistent while adapting to urgency. The GPS "Calm Authority" voice attribute becomes the governing principle — our voice gets calmer as the situation gets more serious.

PhaseTimelineActionOwner
Acknowledge0–30 minInternal holding statement to affected site leadership. "We are aware of [incident type] at [site]. Response teams are activated. Updates will follow at [time]." No speculation. No blame. Facts only.Site Security Mgr
Inform30 min–2 hrsStructured update to GPS Director, Section Heads, and affected site all-staff. Channels: email + digital signage for affected site, email for global leadership.GPS Strategy
Coordinate2–24 hrsCross-functional alignment with Corporate PR (if external exposure), HR (if personnel impact), Legal (if regulatory). GPS provides facts; Corporate PR handles any external statement.GPS Director
Resolve & Debrief24–72 hrsAll-clear communication. After-action summary (internal). Brand voice returns to standard warmth. Lessons learned documented.GPS Strategy + MarCom
GPS never speaks externally. All external communication routes through Corporate PR. GPS provides factual briefing materials. The GPS brand voice adapts under pressure but never abandons its core attributes — especially "Human First" and "Calm Authority."

Visual Consistency 視覺一致性

Three pillars govern visual consistency across all GPS outputs:

Typography
Brand Fonts Only
Stick with the brand fonts. Use approved heading/subheading/body text sizes for hierarchy. Max 3 font sizes in one sector to maintain readability.
Color
Brand Palette
Begin with neutral or brand-approved colors. Sticking to the brand palette makes it easier to keep things consistent without needing design expertise. You may use topic-specific color themes, but avoid harsh neon tones or visually jarring combinations.
Graphics
Pattern for Highlight
Use graphic pattern for highlight, not filler. The checkerboard elements should accent and complement, never overwhelm the content.

Visual Do's & Don'ts 視覺規範

Text Hierarchy

DoUse approved heading/subheading/body text sizes. Follow spacing and alignment structure. Use max 3 different font sizes/weights in one sector to keep readability and hierarchy. Rearrange layout based on your need — the structure is flexible.
Don'tDo not mix fonts or make everything bold. Do not stretch or shrink text manually. Do not center-align everything by default — left-alignment is the standard for body text.

Illustration Style

DoUse minimal, clean, vector-style illustrations. Maintain consistent stroke, perspective, and color. Prefer neutral or brand-accented palettes. Introduce custom topic-specific color accents carefully.
Don'tDo not use mismatched clipart or stock cartoon packs. Do not combine different illustration styles with photo or 3D styles randomly. Maintain a single visual language per piece.

Color Usage

DoStart with neutral or brand base. Ensure contrast between background and type. You may introduce custom topic-specific color accents carefully as long as they harmonize with the brand palette.
Don'tDo not use harsh neon tones or visually jarring combinations. Do not apply random rainbows or unbalanced color blocks. Do not color logos or type arbitrarily without ensuring sufficient contrast.

Brand Violation Remediation 品牌違規處理

When a quarterly brand audit or ad-hoc review identifies a violation, follow this remediation pathway:

MinorWrong font weight, slightly off colorMarCom sends correction email with correct asset attached. No escalation.Corrected within 5 business days
ModerateStretched logo, non-brand color palette, missing clearspaceGPS Strategy contacts site owner directly. Provides correct template. Logged in audit tracker.Corrected within 10 business days

Remediation philosophy: The goal is education, not punishment. Most violations happen because people don't have access to the right templates. Fix the system, not the person.
