01 How We Work Together 我們怎麼合作

Three simple rules:

Sort first, then start

Before making anything, decide how important it is. Big decisions go to the Division Head. Regular work stays between Strategy and Promotion.

Use the checklist, not guesswork

The Brand Bible tells us what's right and wrong. When checking work, refer to the specific chapter — not personal preference.

Protect the Director's time

Only strategic items need the Division Head. Routine work flows between Strategy and Promotion. If in doubt, ask Strategy first.

02 Who Does What 誰負責什麼

Division Head 處長

Sets the direction

Approves big-picture decisions: brand messaging changes, co-branding partnerships, new site brand launch, Director-level messages. Reviews the annual brand health report in Q4.

In practice: You'll see strategic briefs from Strategy a few times per quarter. One round of feedback, then it ships.
Strategy & Transformation 策略轉型

Writes the brief, guards the standard

Writes content briefs. Reviews all campaign-level work for message alignment. Maintains the Brand Bible. Runs quarterly check-ups. Decides which tier a request belongs to.

In practice: Every campaign or new content piece starts with your brief. You review Promotion's work against the Brand Bible before it goes live.
Promotion 推廣

Makes it real

Designs and produces all visual materials. Manages templates. Publishes to platforms. For routine items (posters from templates, standard emails), self-checks against the Brand Bible and publishes directly.

In practice: You bring the brand to life. For everyday items, the Brand Bible checklist is your approval. For campaigns, Strategy reviews before you publish.

03 Three Types of Work 三種工作類型

Not everything needs the same process. Sort each request into one of these:

Big Decisions

Division Head reviews

Brand messaging changes, Director messages, co-branding, new site launch, crisis response.

Timeline: ~10 working days
Flow: Strategy briefs → Division Head approves direction → Promotion produces → Strategy checks → Division Head signs off → publish
Campaigns & Content

Strategy reviews

Newsletters, awareness campaigns, new poster designs, landing page updates, training decks, digital signage, LINE OA learning & news pushes.

Timeline: ~5 working days
Flow: Strategy briefs → Promotion produces → Promotion self-checks visuals → Strategy reviews message → publish
Everyday Items

Promotion self-checks

Posters from approved templates, routine emails, shift briefing cards, minor text updates, asset re-exports.

Timeline: ~2 working days
Flow: Promotion produces → checks against Brand Bible → publish
Not sure which type? Default to the higher one. If you're unsure whether something is "Everyday" or "Campaign" — treat it as a Campaign. Strategy will re-classify if needed.
About LINE OA (@918rtiju): LINE is not an official company channel — it operates outside the firewall. We use it only to push learning content and news to colleagues (non-corporate information). Because it represents GPS brand externally, LINE content always goes through Strategy review before sending. No corporate-sensitive or policy-level content via LINE.

06 When Something's Off 需要修正的時候

Mistakes happen — especially when we're moving fast. The goal isn't blame; it's fixing it quickly and learning from it.

🔧

Small Fix

Wrong font weight, color slightly off, spacing issue

Promotion fixes it within 2 days. Done.
🔄

Needs a Redo

Wrong logo version, missing bilingual text, tone doesn't match

Promotion revises within 5 days. Strategy notes it for the quarterly review.
⏸️

Pause & Discuss

Unauthorized co-branding, off-message content, regulatory risk (e.g. missing OSHA-required ES text)

Pull it down. Strategy + Division Head discuss within 48 hours. Figure out why, then fix the process so it doesn't recur.
The pattern to watch for: If the same type of issue shows up 3+ times in a quarter, it's not a people problem — it's a process problem. Strategy should update the template, the checklist, or the Brand Bible section to prevent it.
